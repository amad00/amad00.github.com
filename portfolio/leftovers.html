Problem

It can be difficult to plan ahead when shopping, and many people end up with excess food that they let go to waste. Some college students don't have the budget for this nor the necessary resources or stability of continuously dining out as opposed to making food at home.

Households are the largest contributors to food waste within the food production and consumption pipeline, contributing approximately 61% of the 931 million tons of food waste generated in 2019 (UN Environment Programme, 2021)*. 

*UN Environment Programme. (2021, March). UNEP Food Waste Index Report 2021. Retrieved January 30, 2022, from https://www.unep.org/resources/report/unep-food-waste-index-report-2021

So how do we go about being more environmentally friendly while making sure we are not only eating healthy but also not bringing detriment to our health because of the lack of knowledge we have of how or what to cook and what we can afford to cook?

Solution

Meal Planning Website

  • Makes use of what the user already has available to them at home 
  • Suggest recipes that fit their needs, preferences, dietary restrictions, and budget 

We decided to do some research on our competitors...

Competitor Analysis

SuperCook

Curates recipes for users based on the ingredients they have selected

BigOven

Allows users to select up to three ingredients to see which meals can be made

SuperCook and BigOven are examples of services for users to make meals based on the ingredients they already have. However, the two websites lack inclusivity and personalization

Survey

We conducted a Google survey to gauge the need for this product and better understand the pain points of our target users

Information gathered:

  • General information about the user and food preferences
  • Site features and preferences

We surveyed 18 people, with the majority of our participants (77.8%) in the 18-24 age range

Survey Results & Insights

Two major motivation reasons for looking for recipes:

  • To find and try something new
  • Hunger & Boredom

In response to whether having to create an account would drive them away from the site without first having a chance to explore 38.8% said yes, 16.7% said no, 44.4% maybe. Given the results, we decided on giving users the option to either make an account or browse as a guest

User Persona

Once we had a better understanding of what we wanted to problem to solve we started working on some sketches

Sketches

We wanted the onboarding section to be a main feature and stand out

We thought a website platform would be best to build our design on. Not all our target users might have a computer or laptop, but they would be able to use ones on campus, or find a public one at a library for example.

Different steps in our onboarding stage, starting from the landing page, to the curated list of recipes generated for you

1. Landing Page

Brief description about site with option to start onboarding process or move straight to the log in page

2. Restrictions

Select your diet preferences (vegan, gluten-free, etc.) and input any allergies

3. Cuisine preferences

Input as many different cuisines as you like

4. Kitchen Appliances

Select all the kitchen appliances you own (and add any not listed)

5.Budget

Select your price range

6. Recipe Filter

Further refine your search with these filters: time, type (breakfast, etc.), and nutrition

6. Ingredients

Input the ingredients that you have

7. Account Pt. 1

Choose whether to save your information by creating an account or continue on as a guest

7. Account Pt. 2

Create an account through google or manually input your information

8. Recipes Page

Your curated list of recipes based on your preferences
